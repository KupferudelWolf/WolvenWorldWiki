**Flux** refers both to elemental energy and the massless accumulation of it that commonly appears as small, colorful, ethereal spheres. Flux varies in size from 0.03 mm orbs meandering in the air, to large round orbs around 20 cm across.

Flux is a necessary component for **spellcasting**. A **conduit** is a person who specializes in working with flux; they are spellcasters. Spellcasting is a learned skill; everybody is capable of being a conduit.

If flux touches something with less flux than its **flux capacity** can hold, then it absorbs into that thing. Flux itself can absorb flux it touches, becoming a larger orb, especially if the flux has a similar size or **color**. Ironically, flux becomes exponentially less likely to merge as its size increases.

## Rules

Flux has physical limitations.
1. Flux cannot be created or destroyed.
2. Flux cannot have 0 or 1 saturation or value. The universal average of both is exactly 0.5 and cannot increase or decrease in a closed system.
3. Flux fills its volume; space cannot be a complete flux vacuum.

## Colors

Flux has **color**: a visual property of flux that denotes what elements it can manipulate.

All flux has hue, saturation, and value.

### Hue
**Hue** is cyclic, corresponding to flux's literal color (red, blue, etc.) as well as its "type". All hues are visible; they correspond to perceived color, not electromagnetic wavelengths.

Flux attracts analogous (similar) hues and repels complimentary (opposing) hues. For example, yellow flux would move away from purple flux but toward orange flux. If complimentary flux manage to touch, they even out their hues and values, and reduce in saturation; in the previous example, if the yellow and purple flux are made to touch, they will equalize and merge into flux of their combined size, very pale in color, perhaps greenish.

Color corresponds directly to the magical properties flux influences. To generalize, flux can be linked to the classical elements as follows:

| Hue | Insinuation | Classical |
| -- | -- | -- |
| Magenta | heat; warmth | fire |
| Yellow | sky | air |
| Cyan | cold | water |
| Purple | ground | earth |

This mnemonic device also helps clarify complimentary colors; heat obviously opposes cold, and sky presumably opposes ground.

### Saturation & Value
The **value** of the flux denotes its density. Darker flux has less power; it will absorb surrounding flux, or else shrink, until its value and size are closer to the surrounding flux.

The **saturation** of flux corresponds to how easy it is to tint. Pale flux is more flexible, while vivid flux is more readily usable.

Both value and saturation can be described as parameters, ranging from 0 to 1. The bounds are limits that the parameters approach; flux cannot be exactly 0 of 1 of value or saturation.

### Tinting
Flux can **tint**, or change color. Everything tints flux a certain color at certain rates.

## Spilling

**Spilling** refers to the emission of flux from someone or something. When flux is spilled, it scatters to the surrounding area.

Flux can be spilled when a conduit casts magic, when magic is casted on a target, or when a conduit fails to actively hold flux. A target can be made to spill flux simply by targeting it with magic.

## Holding

Everything can **passively** hold onto a certain amount of flux. Even inanimate objects naturally absorbs (and sometimes even tints) flux. Everything has a certain **flux capacity** it can passively hold, usually depending on its size. Conduits increase their flux capacity as they improve their spellcasting.

If something is not at capacity, flux naturally gravitates toward it. The less flux it has, the faster the flux moves.

Conduits can **actively** hold more flux than their natural flux capacity can hold. The physical cap to this is exceedingly high and thus trivial. However, a conduit must focus on keeping the flux in their body, else it will spill.

## Spellcasting

A conduit can cast flux at something to cause a magical effect. The **target** of a spell can be an object, another person, or the conduit themself.

When the conduit casts a spell, they emit flux. A hypothetical, purely efficient cast would channel all flux toward the target, resulting in that flux spilling around the target, now colorless. However, this is impossible. At least some of the flux's color will remain, and some flux will spill from the conduit and between them and their target, depending on the conduit's skill. The target also spills some of its own flux, especially if the target is a conduit actively holding extra flux.
