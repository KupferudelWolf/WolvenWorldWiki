Five colors of flux (1d6 dice)
- M (magenta/red) and C (cyan/blue)
- Y (yellow) and K (purple/black)
- W (white)
You have a capacity of each pair of colors. White can be put in either capacity.
You can hold up to double your pool's worth of one color of flux. You cannot hold more than 1× capacity for two opposing colors.
If you are hit, you drop all flux above 1× your pool.

## Capacity
x

At the end of your turn, if you have more than double your capacity of flux, you **explode**. This is the equivalent of the spell Explode Flux being cast directly onto you. (This spell is not considered to be "casted" by any creature.)

## Casting
1. Roll the dice onto the board directly.
    - The space each die lands on is determined by whichever has the most of its corners. You may choose the space if there's a tie.
    - W can't be rolled directly, but can be exchanged for a color while casting.
2. Apply environmental effects. 
    - i.e. some environments may make the rolls of certain colors below a certain threshold 1.
3. Dice of opposing colors on the same space are given -1; any 0s are removed.
4. Consider all non-1s board for the effect (even if they weren't rolled).
    - If no non-1s remain on or within reach of the target, then the spell fails.
    - If the spell was Overcharged, 1s cannot be counted for the total.
5. Leave the 1s on the board.
6. The rest go to the nearest creature's pool.
    - If the creature can't hold that flux, they explode!
